PD-LRRK2-01 · SPECIFIC AIMSNIH R01 · PAGE 1 OF 17

Selective LRRK2 kinase inhibition to slow dopaminergic neurodegeneration in Parkinson's disease

Principal Investigator · Gladstone Institutes · resubmission A1

Gain-of-function mutations in LRRK2 — most commonly G2019S — raise kinase activity roughly 2.6-fold1 and elevate phosphorylation of Rab10 at Thr732, impairing lysosomal clearance in dopaminergic neurons3. We hypothesise that selective, ATP-competitive inhibition of LRRK2 restores lysosomal homeostasis and slows neurodegeneration4.

A 13-week non-human-primate study identified reversible lung phospholipidosis5; this caveat is carried explicitly through the aims below rather than set aside.

AIM 1

Establish central target engagement in a Phase 1b cohort via CSF pRab10 dose-response.

AIM 2

Confirm kinase-dependent lysosomal rescue across an iPSC allelic series.

AIM 3

Define the therapeutic window with intermittent dosing to resolve the lung-safety signal.

Every superscript resolves to a primary source in the evidence appendix. No sentence in this document originates with the model unattributed.
